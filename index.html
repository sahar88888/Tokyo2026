Mon Jul 6 · day-trip · Hakone loop

Hakone

The classic Hakone loop — a mountain train, a cable car, a ropeway over a live volcano, a pirate ship across a caldera lake, and a shrine gate standing in the water, all on one circular route back to where you started. Monday is the ideal day to leave Tokyo (most city museums and gardens are shut anyway). Transport from Shinjuku is already booked & paid.

GoShinjuku → Hakone-Yumoto on the Odakyu Romancecar (~85 min, reserved seats) — already paid. Yumoto is the onsen gateway town: hot-spring foot baths, sweet-shops and the Hayakawa river before you start the loop. Run it counter-clockwise and each leg connects to the next.
TrainHakone Tozan Railway → Gōra (~40 min) — Japan's oldest mountain switchback line, zig-zagging up through the forest at three reversing points. In late June–mid-July it runs as the "ajisai (hydrangea) train," with blue banks of flowers lining the track — you may catch the tail of it.
ArtHakone Open-Air Museum (hop off at Chōkoku-no-Mori, one stop before Gōra) — a hillside sculpture park with Henry Moore bronzes, a whole Picasso pavilion, a stained-glass "Symphonic Sculpture" tower you climb, and a hot-spring foot bath. The rain-proof anchor of the day.
VolcanoGōra → Sōunzan cable car → Ōwakudani ropeway. Ōwakudani (大涌谷, "Great Boiling Valley") is a live volcanic zone left by Hakone's eruptions ~3,000 years ago — sulfur vents, steaming yellow hillsides, the smell of rotten eggs. Buy kuro-tamago, black eggs boiled in the sulfur springs (shells blackened by iron sulfide) — legend says each adds seven years to your life. On a clear day Fuji fills the skyline. The ropeway occasionally closes for volcanic gas — a substitute bus runs; check status that morning.
LakeRopeway down to Tōgendai → pirate-ship cruise on Lake Ashi (芦ノ湖, a caldera lake; ~30–40 min to Moto-Hakone). The mock galleons are kitsch but the ride is lovely, Fuji framed over the water when the cloud lifts.
ShrineMoto-Hakone — Hakone Shrine & the Heiwa-no-Torii. The vermilion torii stands right in the lake (the famous shot — expect a short queue), the shrine itself up a cedar-lined stone stairway in the forest. Walk a stretch of the Old Tōkaidō cedar avenue alongside.
HistoryHakone Sekisho (Checkpoint) at Hakone-machi — a faithful rebuild of the Edo-era barrier station that controlled all traffic on the Tōkaidō road in and out of Edo, with a small museum. A quick, characterful stop by the boat pier.
BackReturn: Hakone Tozan bus from Moto-Hakone to Hakone-Yumoto, then the Romancecar to Shinjuku. Budget ~6–7 hours for the full loop at an unhurried pace — drop the checkpoint or a museum if time runs short.
TanabataIf you've energy left (Jul 6 eve): Zōjōji temple, beside Tokyo Tower, lays out a "Milky Way" of washi-paper candles across the grounds on the evenings of Jul 6–7 for Tanabata (the star festival). It's ~15–20 min from Shinjuku on the way back — a quiet, glowing way to end the day. Optional; skip it if Hakone has wiped you out.
NoteEarly July is rainy season, so Fuji is a bonus, not a promise — the Open-Air Museum and Ōwakudani carry the day even in cloud. Pack a layer (cooler up top) and your passport for tax-free shopping in Yumoto.

Any day · around Yotsuya · walk or one stop

Near your base

Yotsuya sits between Kagurazaka, Akasaka and Shinjuku — a lot is within a short walk or a single stop, ideal for a spare hour, a jet-lagged morning, or a low-key last night. On the map below: orange pins are food, blue pins are sights & activities.

Sights & strolls

any time四谷 · 神楽坂 · 赤坂
From YotsuyaAll of these are ≤ ~15 min on foot or 1–2 stops on the Marunouchi / Namboku / Chūō-Sōbu lines.
PalaceState Guest House — Akasaka Palace (迎賓館赤坂離宮) — a jaw-dropping Neo-Baroque palace (1909), modelled on Versailles & Buckingham, ~10 min walk south. Wander the free-ish front garden and fountain, or book the main-building + garden tour (ticketed). No photos inside; closed Wed, and sometimes shut for state guests — check before you go.
StrollKagurazaka (神楽坂) — a former geisha quarter of cobbled slopes and hidden stone alleys, one stop to Iidabashi. Zenkoku-ji temple anchors the main street; duck into Hyōgo-yokochō's lanes, and see Kengo Kuma's glass-and-wood Akagi Shrine. Loveliest in the early evening.
GardenShinjuku Gyoen (新宿御苑) — one of Tokyo's great gardens: English, French and Japanese landscapes plus a grand greenhouse, ~15 min west. ¥500 (IC card OK); closed Mon. A perfect slow morning.
ToysTokyo Toy Museum (東京おもちゃ美術館) — in a lovely old wooden schoolhouse in Yotsuya, ~10 min: hands-on, beautifully-made wooden toys and games you actually play with, no plastic junk. Charming for any age. Closed Thu; 10:00–16:00.
WaterChidorigafuchi (千鳥ヶ淵) — the green Imperial-moat walk east of you, with rowboats out on the water in season. A calm, leafy stretch beside the palace grounds.
ToriiHie Shrine (日枝神社) in Akasaka — a hillside shrine with its own tunnel of red torii gates (a mini Fushimi-Inari), a couple of stops south. Quiet, photogenic, open from 06:00.
ListenSuntory Hall (Akasaka) — Japan's first purpose-built classical concert hall (1986), vineyard-style seating and superb acoustics. Worth checking the programme for a Bach / symphony night while you're here; they also sell a few day-of tickets from 18:00.

north — your geology wheelhouse

Ueno Park's museum row — the National Museum of Nature & Science (dinosaurs, minerals) is squarely your interest — plus Ameyoko market under the tracks and Shinobazu Pond's summer lotus.

5 · Yanaka + Nezu shortlisted

north — the near-miss

The old low-rise Tokyo: Yanaka Ginza's nostalgic shopping lane, cat backstreets, and Nezu Shrine's tunnel of red torii — a quieter stand-in for Kyoto's Fushimi Inari. A gentle half-day if you swap something out.

7 · Ikebukuro

north-west — otaku

Sunshine City, Otome Road, the Animate flagship, and the largest Pokémon Center. An Akihabara-style geek block if one wasn't enough.

10 · Tsukiji + Hama-rikyu shortlisted

central-bay — the proper tea moment

A Tsukiji Outer Market food crawl (tamagoyaki, uni, skewers), then a walk to Hama-rikyu garden and matcha + a sweet at the Nakajima teahouse over the pond — the drop-in tea moment, no booking.

16 · Kagurazaka

west-central — your special kaiseki pick

Cobblestone slopes, hidden alleys (Hyogo-yokocho), Kuma's modern Akagi Shrine — and kaiseki, the refined multi-course dinner you flagged as a special want. A booked-dinner evening.

17 · Nishi-Azabu / Roppongi

central-west — evening

Gonpachi, the izakaya that inspired the Kill Bill set, for robata skewers and soba; the free Azabudai Hills sky lobby; Tokyo Tower lit up nearby.

18 · Mitaka / Kichijoji

far west — half-day excursion

The Reversible Destiny Lofts — Arakawa's candy-coloured experimental apartments, toured by appointment — plus Inokashira Park's pond and Kichijoji's Harmonica Yokocho alleys. Book a tour date in your window or skip.
